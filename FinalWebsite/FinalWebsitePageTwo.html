Filmography

DeVito’s first major role on the big screen was in the 1975 film One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest where he reprised his role from the 1971 off-Broadway play of the same title. DeVito then went on to star in the hit T.V series Taxi which aired for 5 years. Post his time on Taxi, DeVito began his successful film career by appearing in such films as Terms of Endearment (1983), Romancing the Stone (1984), and its sequel, The Jewel of the Nile (1985), Ruthless People (1986).

In 1987, DeVito made his feature film directing debut with the film Throw Momma from the Train and then went on to direct and co-star in 1989’s The War of the Roses. In 1991, DeVito founded the production company, Jersey Films, which went on to produce blockbuster films Pulp Fiction, Get Shorty, Erin Brockovich, Gattaca, Garden State, Man on the Moon, T.V series Reno 911! and it’s big screen spin-off Reno 911!: Miami. In 1996, DeVito was able to showcase his acting, directing and producing talents when he adapted Roald Dahl’s children book, Matilda. More recently, DeVito can be seen playing Harry Reynolds in the television show Always Sunny in Philadelphia and in December 2019, DeVito will be starring in Jumanji: The Next Level the second sequel to 1995’s Jumanji.
